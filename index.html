Why Drisya

India’s art was never meant to stay flat.

Miniature paintings lived in the hand. Textiles moved with the body. Temples choreographed how we walked and felt. Today we mostly meet them on tiny screens and behind glass. Drisya asks: what if we could step inside again?

The Idea

From scroll to portal.

Drisya transforms Indian visual traditions into immersive spaces using VR, projection and spatial sound— keeping the craft and context, but expanding the way we experience them.

Who it’s for

New eyes on old worlds.

Designed for students, travellers, designers, families and anyone curious about India beyond clichés. It’s not a museum you walk past—it’s one you disappear into.

Origins

Where our worlds begin.

Every room in Drisya is anchored in a real craft, place or community. We are not inventing fantasy India—we’re amplifying what already exists.

Miniature Story Courts

Rajasthan and Deccan ateliers painted love, war and devotion on palm-sized pages. In Drisya those pages become moving walls you can stroll beside.

Textile Rivers

Banarasi, Kanchipuram, Ajrakh, Ikat, Bandhani, Phulkari and Patola form the color and pattern engine of our textile universes.

Folk & Tribal Story Worlds

Warli, Gond, Madhubani and Pattachitra give us characters, forests and graphic languages that guide you through the experience.

Sacred Architecture

Temples, mosques, stepwells and stupas provide the geometry and rhythm for portal spaces and transitions between worlds.

Festival Color & Ritual

Rangoli, Holi pigments, lamps and processions inform the light, sound and atmosphere that hold everything together.

Inside NMACC

The five immersive worlds of Drisya.

Spread across the Grand Theatre, the Art House, the Cube, the Lobby and the Promenade, Drisya turns India’s leading cultural centre into a cluster of portals.

World 01 · Grand Theatre

Origins Hall — the living fresco.

A 360° cosmic mural where miniatures, folk art and constellations of textiles wrap the entire theatre—floor to ceiling—into one continuous, slowly shifting painting.

World 02 · Art House

Threads of India — inside the cloth.

A VR textile universe. Banarasi zari, Ajrakh indigo, Ikat gradients and Phulkari embroidery flow around you as rivers of fabric you can follow, pause and inspect stitch by stitch.

World 03 · The Cube

Painted Stories — folk worlds in motion.

In a dark, glowing chamber, Warli, Gond and Madhubani lines animate gently: animals breathe, figures move, patterns listen and respond to your presence.

World 04 · Lobby Portal

Sacred Spaces — walking through light.

Temple towers, arches and stepwell geometry are recomposed as a luminous gateway; you literally walk through carved light into the rest of Drisya.

World 05 · Promenade

Corridor of Journeys — the long story.

A gently curving corridor where walls act as a continuous scroll, linking regions, crafts and eras as you move—your walk becomes the line that connects them.

How it works

Your visit in three simple steps.

1

Arrive & tune in

Check in at NMACC and choose your time slot and language. A short intro film orients you to the art forms behind what you’re about to see.

2

Choose your worlds

Move from Origins Hall into textiles, stories, sacred spaces or the corridor. Stay in one world or wander; each room is designed to reward slowing down.

3

Take it home

Scan QR codes to save artworks, craftspeople and regions to your phone via Google Arts & Culture—so Drisya continues long after you leave the building.
