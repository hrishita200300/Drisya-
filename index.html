IMMERSIVE CULTURAL WORLDS

A NEW WAY TO SEE INDIA

DRISYA is a VR museum where Indian paintings, textiles, architecture and folk traditions become immersive worlds you can walk through at the NMACC in Mumbai.

Why We Are Creating Drisya

Indian art was never meant to stay flat. Paintings lived in the hand, textiles moved with the body, temples shaped how we walked and felt. Today we mostly see them on screens. Drisya asks:

“What if you could step inside the art instead?”

Where Indian Art Originated

Miniature Story Courts

Rajasthan & Deccan ateliers painted love, war and devotion on palm-sized pages. In Drisya these become moving story walls.

Textile Rivers

Banarasi, Ajrakh, Ikat, Phulkari, Patola — transformed into worlds of flowing cloth.

Folk & Tribal Worlds

Warli, Madhubani, Gond & Pattachitra become animated narrative environments.

Sacred Architecture

Stepwells, temples, mosques & arches become luminous VR portals.

Festival Color

Rangoli, Holi pigments & ritual patterning power our color & light engine.

The Five Worlds of DRISYA at NMACC

01 — Origins Hall

A 360° living fresco made from Indian art traditions.

02 — Threads of India

A VR textile universe filled with Banarasi, Ikat, Ajrakh & more.

03 — Painted Stories

Warli, Gond & Madhubani animations that move with you.

04 — Sacred Spaces

Temple geometry becomes a glowing entry portal.

05 — Corridor of Journeys

A long scroll-like passage linking India’s many visual worlds.

How Your Visit Works

Step 1 — Arrive

Check in, choose your time slot, and tune into a short intro film.

Step 2 — Explore Worlds

Move freely between the five immersive portals inside NMACC.

Step 3 — Take It Home

Scan QR codes to save artworks to your phone via Google Arts & Culture.
